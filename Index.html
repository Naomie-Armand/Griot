How to Make Haitian Griot

Haitian cuisine is rich with history, flavor, and resilience, and no dish represents that better than Griot . Made by marinating pork in citrus, herbs, and spices before simmering and frying it to perfection, this dish is a centerpiece of gatherings across Haiti. It’s traditionally served with Pikliz and fried plantains, creating a balance of spicy, savory, and sweet.
I chose to share this recipe not only because of how meaningful it is to my family, but also to help more people appreciate the beauty of Haitian food and culture. Through food, we celebrate our roots and invite others to experience a small piece of what makes Haiti vibrant and unforgettable.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, seeded & chopped
  • 1 Maggi bouillon cube
  • 2 cups water or chicken broth
  • Oil for frying

Directions

  1. In a bowl, mix pork with lime juice, vinegar, salt, pepper, thyme, garlic, onion, and peppers.
  2. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  3. Transfer to a pot, add water or broth, and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer 45 minutes until pork is tender.
  5. Remove pork, drain, and pat dry with paper towels.
  6. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  7. Fry pork pieces until golden and crispy, about 5–7 minutes.
  8. Drain on paper towels. Serve hot with pikliz and fried plantains.
